# Precedent Transactions

## Overview

Precedent Transactions (M&A Comps) analyze **prices paid** in actual acquisitions. These multiples typically exceed trading comps due to the **control premium** (20-40%).

## Why M&A Multiples Are Higher

```
M&A Multiple > Trading Multiple
```

| Factor | Premium Range | Reason |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| Control premium | 20-40% | Buyer gains voting control + strategic benefits |
| Synergy premium | 10-30% | Cost savings, revenue synergies |
| Illiquidity premium | 5-15% | Private companies trade at a discount to public |
| Auction dynamics | 0-20% | Competitive bidding inflates price |

## Key M&A Multiples

| Multiple | Usage | Typical Range |
|----------|-------|---------------|
| EV/EBITDA | Most common in LBO/M&A | 6×−20× depending on sector |
| EV/Revenue | Pre-revenue targets | 1×−10× |
| P/E | Minority stake purchases | 15×−30× |
| Price/PBV | Asset-heavy industries | 0.8×−2.5× |

## M&A Multiples by Sector (Illustrative 2024-2025)

| Sector | Median EV/EBITDA | Median Control Premium |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Technology | 18.5× | 32% |
| Healthcare | 16.2× | 28% |
| Financial Services | 14.8× | 25% |
| Consumer | 11.5× | 22% |
| Industrial | 10.2× | 20% |
| Energy | 8.5× | 18% |
| Real Estate | 20.3× | 15% |

## Step-by-Step M&A Comps

### Step 1: Identify Relevant Transactions
- Same industry
- Similar size (50%−2× target revenue)
- Recent (last 2-3 years)
- Public or private target

### Step 2: Collect Transaction Data
- Transaction value (including assumed debt)
- Target's LTM EBITDA at announcement
- Control premium over unaffected stock price

### Step 3: Calculate Implied Multiples

```
Implied EV/EBITDA = (Transaction Value) / (Target EBITDA)
```

### Step 4: Apply to Target

```
Implied Offer = Target EBITDA × Median M&A Multiple
Bid Range = Implied Offer ± 10% (negotiation room)
```

## Private Company Discount

Private companies typically trade at a **20-30% discount** to public comps due to:
- Illiquidity
- Less disclosure/transparency
- Limited buyer pool

```
Private Multiple = Public Multiple × (1 − Illiquidity Discount)
```

## EquiValue Relevance

M&A comps are less actionable for our app (we're a retail investor tool, not an M&A advisory). However, they provide:
- **Upper bound** for valuation (no retail investor should pay LBO prices)
- **Market sanity check** — "Could this company be sold for this price?"
